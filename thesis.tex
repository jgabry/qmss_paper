% ABSTRACT
\begin{abstract}
\begin{quote}
\begin{changemargin}{.5cm}{.5cm}
\justify

With reanalyses of two well-known studies in American political development, 
\citeA{wawro_designing_2014} provide empirical support for their position that 
traditional and current usage of probabilistic modeling for historical social scientific 
inquiry is insufficient for describing and making inferences about characteristics 
central to successful qualitative historical analyses. In this thesis, an empirical 
analysis is conducted demonstrating the merits of Katznelson and Wawro's 
recommendations for bridging the quantitative vs. qualitative divide. Considerable 
space is given to a formal description of the proposed statistical models, as well 
as a discussion of estimation using Stan's implementation of Hamiltonian Monte 
Carlo. 
\end{changemargin}
\end{quote}
\end{abstract}
